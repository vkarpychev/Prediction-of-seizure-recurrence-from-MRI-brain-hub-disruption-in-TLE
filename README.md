Patients with temporal lobe epilepsy (TLE) can achieve seizure freedom in the early period after surgery, yet up to half experience seizure recurrence in the following years (i.e., long-term). TLE is associated with disruption of highly connected brain regions (hubs), which may reduce the likelihood of long-term surgical success. Here, we tested whether disruption of physiological (normative) hubs predicts long-term seizure outcomes.
